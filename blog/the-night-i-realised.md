It was late. The kind of late where the only people still awake are the ones who can't sleep and the ones who don't want to.

My phone buzzed. A familiar name. A familiar voice when I picked up — and something about hearing it at that hour, in that quiet, made everything feel different. More true.

We talked for a while. Not about anything important, in the way most conversations are measured. No big announcements. No crisis to navigate. Just catching up, filling in the gaps, laughing about something small.

But somewhere in the middle of that call, I realised: **this** is why I'm here.

Not the career. Not the degree. Not the plans that look impressive on paper. Those things matter — I'm not dismissing them. But what actually moves me, what fills the space between tasks and goals and deadlines, is this. Connection. Familiarity. The sound of someone who knows you and is glad you picked up.

> One late call, one familiar voice, and a reminder of why I'm here.

I'd been grinding for weeks. Head down, focused, checking things off. And there's a version of that which is healthy — purposeful, disciplined. But I'd been doing it in a way that was slightly hollow. Moving through the days but not quite touching them.

That call touched something.

After we hung up, I sat for a while with the phone in my hand. The apartment was quiet. I thought about what matters. I thought about who I want to call at midnight when I can't sleep. I thought about who picks up.

Most of the big things I'm building are, ultimately, in service of those small things. The good work, the late nights, the ambition — they're all pointing toward a life with more room for the people and moments that make it worth living.

It took a phone call at midnight to remind me.
